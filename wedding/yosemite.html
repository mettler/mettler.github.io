Yosemite

Transportation

Here is some excellent National Park Service info on planning your visit, including maps, nearby airports, and driving directions.

Yosemite Valley is a 5 hour drive from San Francisco (this can take longer in heavy traffic). In addition, the weather in March unpredictable, so cars will need to carry snow chains and be prepared to use them. Shops where you can purchase chains are conveniently located on the way to Yosemite (more info here).

The closest airports to Yosemite are Fresno (FAT - 2.5-hours), Modesto (MOD - 2 hours), and Merced (MCE - 2 hours). Sacramento (SAC) is a four-hour drive, and Bay Area airports (SFO, OAK, SJO) are all a five-hour drive.

Public transportation is not easy, but possible - here are some instructions for getting to Yosemite from various airports.

Various shuttles run from the San Francisco area to Yosemite, including StarLine Tours (daily) and California Parlor Car Tours (Tues, Thurs, Sat, Sun).

Let us know if we can help you arrange a carpool.

Hotel reservations fine print

All reservations must be made at least 30 days prior to arrival. One night's deposit is due when booking a room. Additional nights may be paid upon arrival.

Once a reservation has been booked, you may adjust that reservation up to 7 days prior to the arrival date (name, number of guests, arrival & departure dates, or cancellation). Re-booking and re-charging rooms to different credit cards is not permitted.

Reserved rooms require a 7-day advance cancellation or change notice for refund. Cancellations or changes received less than 7 days prior to arrival will automatically forfeit the first night deposit.

There are other options on the main Yosemite lodging page:

  • The Ahwahnee: ceremony location, fancy-schmancy, awesome six-foot fireplaces
  • Curry Village: heated or unheated tent cabins, now hantavirus-free, the cheapest option in the park, and a fun adventure. Note, the cabins are only open weekends, so you would need a proper hotel room for Sunday night. The thrill-seeking or cold-tolerant among you may want to consider the temperature-based booking rates.
  • Tenaya Lodge: about a 45-minute drive outside the park, dog-friendly

Getting around the park

You can drive everywhere you'll need to go, but the Yosemite Valley shuttle runs a loop around the valley floor and stops at or near all accommodations and major vistas around the park. This shuttle operates all year from 7 am to 10 pm, and it will also run later for us on the wedding night so you don't have to worry about driving.

Activities

Yosemite in the winter is gorgeous, and the crowds are far less maddening than during the summer. Here are some possibilities:

  • Take a day hike. Ask the park rangers for trail recommendations given the weather conditions
  • Ice skating at Curry Village
  • Snowshoeing
  • Cross-country (and some amusing downhill) skiing at Badger Pass
  • Go sightseeing around the valley (Yosemite Falls, El Capitan, Half Dome, etc.)
  • Sit in front of the fireplaces at the Ahwahnee playing card/board games or reading
